Interesting Facts About Armadillos

What the heck is that armored shrew thing?

Fun Fact #1: The name "Armadillo" translates from Spanish for "Little Armored One"

This is one of those times when you learn what something means and realizes that some times people who name things can be lazy or uncreative. However, this makes sense since Armadillos are native to parts of Central and South America, where Spanish is mainly spoken.

Actually, my experiences with Armadillos consists mostly of seeing them as roadkill lke every 500 feet while driving around in Mexico. That was probably the inspiration for the pose the armadillo sculpture seen above. I mean, have you seen Rango? It's literally a joke that they love getting hit by cars or something. Which leads us to our next fact!

Fun Fact #2: Nine-banded Armadillos jump three feet into the air as a defense mechanism

Author's Note: I literally laughed so hard when I read this that my roomate thought I was being murdered in my room from the way I was screaming and then crying. With laughter, of course.

After learning this, it makes so much more sense that I see so many just squashed on the side of the road. They really see a car coming and think, "If I jump, I won't die." If they literally did the opposite of that, they would be safe. Why do people talk about lemmings when armadillos are constantly either trying to kill themselves or commit insurance fraud.
